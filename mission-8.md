### Part 1 — Look How Far

Wendy sat at her machine, relaxed now, the way you sit somewhere familiar.

"Show yourself something," Ulysses said. "Type this."

```
history
```

**What you'll see:** every command you've run, numbered, scrolling past — `whoami`, `cowsay`, `ollama run`, `nano ask.py`, all of it. A whole journey, in one list.

Wendy read it in silence. "I did all of that."

"You did all of that," said Ulysses.

### Part 2 — The Elephant, Turned Inside Out

"There's one fear left," Alex said. "But a new kind. Not *'am I behind?'* — it's *'what if I hit something I don't know yet?'*"

Wendy considered it. "…Yeah. There's always going to be something new."

"There is. And here's what changed: you now have a sentence for it."

*"I've learned difficult things before. I can learn this too."*

### Part 3 — What's Beyond

"You don't need it today, but it helps to know the road keeps going." Alex gestured at the landscape from Mission 0.

"When a model outgrows your laptop, there are **GPU workstations** and **cloud machines** you can rent by the hour — the same terminal, just a more powerful computer on the other end. The commands you learned here work *there*, unchanged."

> **💡 Why it works — Your skills are portable up the whole ladder**
> A cloud or GPU machine is reached with **SSH** — a command that opens a terminal on a *remote*
> computer as if it were in front of you. And that remote computer almost always runs… Ubuntu.
> So `ls`, `cd`, `apt install`, `python3`, `git clone` — *every command you learned* — works there
> exactly the same. This is the secret nobody tells beginners: **you already speak the language of
> every machine up the ladder.** Bigger hardware is just a bigger room in the same house.

### Part 4 — The Real Threshold

"There's one last thing to do," Ulysses said. "And it isn't a command."

He nodded toward the door, where someone new had wandered in — staring at their own screen, six browser tabs open, that familiar overwhelmed look.

"Someone just asked the question you asked in Mission 1. Go on."

Wendy walked over. She remembered *exactly* how it felt. She smiled, pulled up a chair, and said the words that define this whole community:

> *"Come on. I'll show you. It's easier than it looks."*

### ✅ What You Learned — Across the Whole Journey

- **M1** — Ubuntu runs inside Windows (WSL); the terminal is a doorway, not a test.
- **M2** — `apt` installs anything, cleanly and reversibly.
- **M3** — real AI runs *locally*, private and free.
- **M4** — you can navigate, create, and edit — the terminal is home.
- **M5** — you write and run Python.
- **M6** — you clone from GitHub; the whole open-source world is reachable.
- **M7** — you build: Python + AI into a program that's yours.
- **M8** — your skills carry to every machine up the ladder — and you can now guide others.

### 📖 Words You Now Know

- **`history`** — the list of commands you've run.
- **SSH** — open a terminal on a remote computer.
- **Cloud / GPU machine** — a more powerful computer you rent and reach with the same commands.
- **Mentor** — someone who remembers what it felt like not to know.

### 🧭 Where To Go Next

*No troubleshooting this time — just doors.* Any of these uses tools you already have:

- **Go deeper on Python** — build a slightly bigger script; ask your local AI to set you a small challenge.
- **Explore models** — `ollama list`, try a different small model, compare their answers.
- **Make something real** — a note-taker, a study helper, a tiny tool for a hobby. Start with `ask.py` and grow it.
- **Push your own project to GitHub** — the reverse of Mission 6; your local AI can walk you through `git init`, `commit`, and `push`.
- **When you outgrow the laptop** — rent a cloud machine, `ssh` in, and notice: it's the same terminal, all the way up.
